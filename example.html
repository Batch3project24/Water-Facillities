WATER RESOURCES

Introduction:

Water is one of the natural resources. It is an essential source for the existence of life on the planet earth.Water Resources are natural resources of water that are potentially useful for humans. It is widely used for various purposes such as drinking, washing, bathing, cleaning, cooking, irrigation, and other industrial and domestic purposes. All living things need water for their survival. We cannot imagine life without water.On average, an individual uses 600 to 700 litres in a day.

Water resources are fundamental to life, ecosystems, and human development. They include various forms such as surface water, groundwater, and atmospheric water, each playing a crucial role in sustaining life, agriculture, industry, and recreation.


Types of Water Resources:

1.Surface Water

2.Ground water

3.Atmospheric water

1.Suraface Water:

Surface water refers to natural water bodies like rivers, lakes, and reservoirs. These are easily accessible sources for drinking, agriculture, and industrial processes. Surface water is renewed through the hydrological cycle, primarily through precipitation and runoff. Major rivers and lakes often serve as the primary sources of water for urban areas and agricultural regions.


2.Ground Water:

Groundwater is stored in aquifers, which are underground layers of water-bearing rock or sediment. It is a crucial source of water for irrigation and drinking, especially in arid and semi-arid regions where surface water is scarce. Groundwater extraction must be managed sustainably to prevent depletion and contamination.


3.Atmospheric Water:

Atmospheric water includes all forms of precipitation, such as rain, snow, sleet, and fog. It is a key component of the hydrological cycle, replenishing surface and groundwater sources. Atmospheric water is harnessed through methods like rainwater harvesting, which can provide an additional source of water in regions with adequate rainfall.
